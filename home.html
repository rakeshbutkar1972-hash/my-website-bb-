What is AES?

AES is a specific symmetric encryption algorithm adopted as a standard by the U.S. government and widely used worldwide. It encrypts data in fixed-size blocks (128 bits) using keys of 128, 192, or 256 bits. AES applies a series of substitution, permutation, and mixing operations to secure the data.

Key Points:
Symmetric: Same key is used for both encryption and decryption.
Highly secure: Resistant to known attacks when used with proper key size.
Fast and efficient, suitable for software and hardware applications.
Commonly used in:
Secure messaging (WhatsApp, Signal)
Banking transactions
VPNs and HTTPS communication
